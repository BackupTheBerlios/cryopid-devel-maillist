On Fri, 2008-05-16 at 15:28 -0400, Jerod Sikorskyj wrote:
> Dear anyone who can help,
> 
> I am attempting to compile Cryopid on Redhat with the following uname
> info:
> 
> Linux bioinfo4.niagara.edu 2.4.21-9.ELsmp #1 SMP Thu Jan 8 17:08:56
> EST 2004 i686 i686 i386 GNU/Linux

That means that your tool chain (gcc, ld, ar, etc) are also from 2004
and will not understand some of the compiler settings.

My first inclination would be to simply upgrade your tool chain to a
more recent version. 

> 
> I followed the installation readme, and typed "make" while in the /src
> directory.  Here is the error message I got:
> 
> [root at bioinfo4 src]# make
> ! test -h arch && rm -f arch && ln -s arch-i386 arch || true
> make -C arch 'CFLAGS+=-DUSE_TCPCP -fno-stack-protector'
> make[1]: Entering directory `/root/cryopid-0.5.9.1/src/arch-i386'
> cc -DUSE_TCPCP -fno-stack-protector -g -Wall -Os -fpic -I. -I..   -c
> -o stub.o stub.c
> cc1: unrecognized option `-fno-stack-protector'
> make[1]: *** [stub.o] Error 1
> make[1]: Leaving directory `/root/cryopid-0.5.9.1/src/arch-i386'
> make: *** [arch] Error 2

What version of gcc are you using, exactly? iirc, protection from stack
smashing was introduced before the option to turn it off
(no-stack-protector).

You might be able to just remove that particular flag safely with your
compiler. The worst thing that would happen is a nasty glibc error
complaining that stack smashing was detected.

Note, even with it removed, you might run into other issues due to the
age of your tool chain.
