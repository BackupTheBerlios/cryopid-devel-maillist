I think this ties in a bit with future features .. if you feel that cryopid might take
advantage of 2.6.x-level features in the near future, we should just focus on 2.6.x as a
target, but it would be very useful to have 2.4.x support as well.  It really depends on
how you see the codebase changing in the future - what features you want to add, and
whether this would get in the way of simply maintaining 2.4.x compatability.  If the push
comes to shove, you could always just maintain a 2.4.x-compatible patchset and expose it
through the sharesource site .. testing when major code changes require, of course.

FWIW, I'm using cryopid as a way of understanding process control from user space, and in
that effort it is very handy.  But from a 'snapshot'-oriented production application of
cryopid, I imagine 2.4.x support is definitely useful.  Lots of production systems still
use a 2.4.x kernel for various reasons, in my experience ..
