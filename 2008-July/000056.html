I just discovered "checkpointing" and it is awesome. At first I
wondered why I had not seen it before, but then I realized that all of
the projects are dead. Well, except for BLCR, but it is not as elegant
as CryptoPID.

Anyway, CryptoPID did not compile on my Gentoo system because
<linux/user.h> does not exist. I copied user.h and page.h from Debian
Stable linux-kernel-headers (2.6.18-7) into /usr/include/linux/ and
/usr/include/asm/. With this addition, CryptoPID compiles fine and
works so far.

Interestingly, this is with both HIGH_RES_TIMERS and NO_HZ, contrary
to the Ubuntu bug report.

The same changes did not allow CryptoID to compile on my AMD64 Debian
Unstable box, which continues to produce compiler errors.

I'll try to find a cleaner solution, and then have a go at getting the
GTK portion working.
