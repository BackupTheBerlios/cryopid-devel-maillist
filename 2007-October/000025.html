I just had exactly the same problem on almost exactly the same setup.
(I've just taken delivery of a new Dell box with Ubuntu pre-installed.
Bizarrely, it has gcc but not stdio.h by default...) Unlike you, I
didn't bother to check whether "zlib.h" was there somewhere but just
blindly grabbed the package anyway:

  sudo apt-get install zlib1g-dev

That solved my problem, as I recall. It's possible that "zlib.h" just
wasn't in the place that CryoPID expected, or maybe there is some
significant difference in our setups.
